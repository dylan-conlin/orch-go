### Perspective is Structural

Each level in a hierarchy exists to provide the external viewpoint that the level below cannot have about itself.

**The test:** "Does this level provide perspective that the level below structurally cannot have?"

**What this means:**

- Hierarchies aren't primarily about authority or delegation
- Each level sees patterns invisible from inside the level below
- The value of "stepping up" is perspective, not power
- **Perspective is a function of context-scoping.** A worker *can* perform synthesis if provided with multiple frames to compare; the orchestrator's unique role is deciding *which* frames to load.

**Action authority collapses perspective.** The moment a meta-level observer gets tools to act on the system, it collapses into being another actor. Observation and action compete for the same cognitive resources. Three attempts at a meta-observer role proved this: a skill with full tools became a dispatch machine, a pattern-matching plugin couldn't interpret what it saw, and a separate session with no action authority caught a tactical loop the primary orchestrator couldn't see. The constraint that preserves perspective is the inability to act.

**What this rejects:**

- "Meta-orchestrator is just orchestrator with more authority" (misses the point)
- "We can collapse levels if we give enough context" (context ≠ perspective)
- "Just remind agents to step back" (reminders operate inside the frame)
- **Treating synthesis as a "higher reasoning" task.** Synthesis is a task that requires cross-frame context.
- "Give the observer tools so it can help" (tools collapse the observer into an actor)
