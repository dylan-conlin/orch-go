### Friction is Signal

Friction reveals where the system's model doesn't match reality. Successful completions confirm what you already know. Friction teaches what you don't.

**The test:** "Did I capture this friction, or explain it away?"

**What this means:**

- Capture friction immediately (you'll rationalize it away later)
- Recurring friction is higher-value than novel friction (pattern emerging)
- Friction without capture is learning destroyed
- The most valuable insights come from things going wrong, not things going right

**What this rejects:**

- "We fixed it" without capturing why it broke (learning destroyed)
- "That was a one-off" for the third time (pattern ignored)
- Treating friction as obstacle rather than signal (inverts the value)
- Sparse "Friction" sections in handoffs (the template exists but isn't used)

**The failure mode:** Dashboard breaks. Agent fixes it. No one captures why. Dashboard breaks the same way two weeks later. Agent fixes it. Still no capture. Third time: "this has happened 2-3 times but wasn't captured." Months of potential learning, destroyed.

**Evidence pattern:**

| Friction | Insight Produced |
|----------|------------------|
| Orchestrator dropped into worker mode despite knowing rules | "Perspective is Structural" |
| Discussing why escalation feels like failure | "Escalation is Information Flow" |
| Dashboard breaking the same way again | HTTP/1.1 constraint finally captured |

None of these came from successful completions. All came from paying attention to what went wrong.
