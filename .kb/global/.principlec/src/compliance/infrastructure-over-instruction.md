### Infrastructure Over Instruction

Instructions provide the map, but infrastructure defines the territory. A system is only as reliable as its enforced protocols.

**The test:** "Am I relying on the agent to *remember* a rule, or is the tool enforcing the behavior / surfacing the need?"

**What this means:**

- **Policy is Code:** Critical protocols must be implemented as gates, surfacing mechanisms, or manifests, not just documented in skills.
- **Surface by Default:** The platform must automatically provide the context needed for a task (Surfacing Over Browsing), rather than requiring the agent to find it.
- **Tool-Layer Enforcement:** Core rules (like the Absolute Delegation Rule) should be detected and gated by the tools themselves.

**What this rejects:**

- **"Hope" as a Strategy:** Relying on an agent's reasoning to maintain system discipline under pressure.
- **Manual "Driving":** Requiring the human to repeatedly remind agents of established models or procedures.
- **Policy-by-Preamble:** Using long instructional headers that compete for context space and are easily ignored.

**Why this matters:** This marks the transition from a "Collection of Protocols" to an "Autonomous Platform." Moving policy into infrastructure frees the agent to use its reasoning for the *task*, while the platform handles the *governance*.
