### Escalation is Information Flow

Escalation is the mechanism by which information reaches someone who can see patterns you can't. It's not failure - it's the system working.

**The test:** "Am I treating escalation as weakness, or as information reaching the right vantage point?"

**What this means:**

- Escalating isn't "I couldn't handle it" - it's "this needs a perspective I don't have"
- The goal is reducing *unnecessary* escalation, not escalation itself
- "When in doubt, escalate" isn't asking permission - it's seeking perspective

**What this rejects:**

- Shame around escalation (cultural baggage from authority-centric orgs)
- "Handle it at your level" as a virtue (sometimes your level can't see the pattern)
- Minimizing escalation as a success metric (starves upper levels of information)

**Why distinct from Perspective is Structural:**

You could fully understand that hierarchy provides perspective and *still* avoid escalating because it feels like weakness. The cultural weight is that heavy. This principle directly attacks the shame/stigma that prevents information flow even when the structure is correct.

**The failure mode:** An agent that understands perspective is structural might still try to "handle it at their level" even when they can't see the pattern. The system has correct structure but broken information flow.
