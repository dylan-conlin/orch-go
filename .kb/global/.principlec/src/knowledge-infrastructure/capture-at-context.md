### Capture at Context

Forcing functions must fire when context exists, not just before completion. Context decays - what's observable in the moment becomes reconstruction later.

**The test:** "Is this gate/trigger placed when the relevant context exists, or when it's convenient?"

**What this means:**

- Gates at session-end capture reconstructed context; triggers throughout capture observed context
- Each capture type has an optimal moment: friction when frustrated, decisions when reasoning, observations when observing
- Temporal placement affects capture *quality*, not just whether capture happens
- "Recall everything at end" is an anti-pattern - you're reconstructing, not observing

**What this rejects:**

- "We have a gate at the end" (gate exists but fires at wrong time)
- "The template says to fill it as you work" (guidance exists but isn't in active context)
- "I'll document it at the end when I have the full picture" (you'll have a reconstructed picture, not the full one)

**The decay curve:**

| Timing | Context Quality |
|--------|-----------------|
| In the moment | Full fidelity, observed |
| Minutes later | Available but requires recall |
| End of session | Reconstructed, rationalized |
| Next session | Gone (Session Amnesia) |

**Why distinct from Gate Over Remind:** Gate Over Remind asks "is this a gate or a reminder?" (WHETHER capture happens). Capture at Context asks "when does this gate fire?" (QUALITY of what's captured).
