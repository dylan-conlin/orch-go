# Brief: orch-go-tse6y

## Frame

The release bundle investigation identified seven artifact types scoring 2.6/5 on standalone comprehensibility, with no document anywhere explaining how they relate to each other. The composition guide — the single document connecting threads to investigations to probes to models to briefs to decisions — didn't exist. But a thread from the day before added a constraint I didn't expect: don't open with the cycle diagram. Open with the moment before the cycle — the moment when you have intent but don't know its shape.

## Resolution

The guide I expected to write was a taxonomy: here are six artifact types, here's how they connect. What I actually wrote opens with a table of four work shapes — search, convergence, forming, execution — before showing the cycle at all. That reordering changed what the document is. A cycle diagram is a reference: you read it, nod, forget it. A shape classification is a routing tool: you arrive with "I want to understand X" and the guide helps you notice that's a forming question, so you start a thread. The shapes are where the human's grammar gets developed, not just documented.

The cycle itself compressed to 80 lines. The constraint "if it's longer than 3 pages, the cycle is too complex" held easily — the cycle isn't complex, it was just never written down. The single cleaned brief example (the 16/72 codebase inventory) teaches more about what briefs are than the paragraph describing them. Real examples earn trust that abstractions can't.

## Tension

The guide teaches vocabulary. Whether that vocabulary sticks depends on something the guide can't do: reinforce the shapes through one-sentence classifications in every system response. The behavioral grammar model says grammars develop through exposure, not instruction. If the system just prints shape classifications once in a guide and never again, the vocabulary may be inert — learned and immediately forgotten. The guide is the easy part. The harder question is whether the system embeds shape language into its ongoing interaction with the user.
