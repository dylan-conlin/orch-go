# Brief: orch-go-3knwo

## Frame

Two threads converged last session with new comprehension surfaces — shape classification ("what kind of move is this?") and resistance signal ("is this working or fighting you?"). The minimum comprehension surface had just landed on three identity-defining elements (threads, briefs, tensions). The question: are we now at five? That feels like the kind of scope expansion that dissolves the product triangle into a feature list. But the signals are real — they answer questions only the system can see.

## Resolution

I expected to find that shape and resistance were either home surface elements or not. The turn: the five-elements thread had already converged to "three, not five" — but its reasoning was subtly wrong. It said the thinking surface should be "purely epistemic" and classified shape/resistance as action signals. They're not. "This looks like a search" doesn't tell you what to do — it tells you what kind of situation you're in. That's comprehension about comprehension.

The resolution is neither five elements nor exclusion. Shape and resistance are computed enrichments that flow through the existing three elements. Shape appears as a one-sentence annotation in orient: "Shape: convergence — 3 related issues ready, 1 thread converging." Resistance surfaces as system-generated tension text: "spawn area showing bolted-on resistance — 4 fixes in 14 days, fix count growing." The home stays at three elements, but those elements start saying things only the system can see. All the data already exists in beads, entropy, and routing — this is a composition problem, not an instrumentation problem.

The design principle comes from behavioral grammar Claim 7: the coupling should develop the human's grammar. You learn to see work shape not because someone explained the taxonomy, but because the system said "this looks like search" fifty times in context.

## Tension

The computed enrichment approach depends on classification accuracy that hasn't been measured. A wrong "bolted-on" label erodes trust faster than a missed one. And there's a deeper question: do computed tensions feel natural alongside human-written tensions, or does the mixed voice create a "who's talking?" dissonance? The design says start subtle (high-confidence only, orient first) — but the real test is whether Dylan says "the system told me something I couldn't see" within two weeks.
