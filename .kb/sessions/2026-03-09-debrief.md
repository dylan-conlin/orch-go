## Session Synthesis

**Thread:** This session was about whether the harness engineering and knowledge physics findings have implications beyond code quality — whether they say something about AI safety that the field isn't seeing.

**Insight:** The compliance/coordination distinction is the sharpest contribution. The controlled coordination demo (N=10 simple + N=1 complex) proved that coordination failure is structural and model-independent: both models scored perfectly in isolation, 100% conflict rate on merge, identical commit messages. This isn't a finding about code — it's a finding about what happens when you scale correct individual behavior without coordination infrastructure. The standard AI safety narrative ("make agents follow instructions better") addresses compliance. Coordination failure gets *worse* with capability. The paperclip problem reframed through coordination failure is more realistic and more dangerous than the compliance version.

**Position:** Dylan now has empirical evidence for a claim the safety community hasn't articulated: aligned agents composing into misaligned systems is the durable problem, and it scales with capability. The publication draft exists (~2800 words, Path B framing). KB-CLI is release-ready (7 blockers fixed, --extract-models shipped). The --model knowledge gate is live and measuring.
