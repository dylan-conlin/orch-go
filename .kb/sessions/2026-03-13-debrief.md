## Session Synthesis

**Thread:** This session was about re-examining the principles after the harness engineering and knowledge accretion work changed what the system means. The trigger was recognizing that the principles document — the most durable artifact in the system — didn't reflect the compliance/coordination distinction, the measurement-enforcement pairing, or the creation/removal asymmetry.

**Insight:** The reorganization revealed something deeper than a document restructuring. Tracing the arc of the last 6 days exposed a single story: publication attempt → trust break (closed-loop spiral) → pivot to measurement → compliance ceiling discovered → coordination recognized as where value lives → principles reorganized around that distinction. Then, reading Ostrom's polycentric governance framework against the system produced the session's sharpest insight: the system is polycentric governance of shared commons (codebase, knowledge, context, attention), where the knowledge pipeline is how ephemeral agents participate in collective choice. "Gates creating gates" — governance infrastructure produces the signal that evolves its own governance, through the friction → kb → decision → principle → infrastructure pipeline. This reconciles Session Amnesia with Ostrom's Principle 3 (the governed participate in rule-making).

**Position:** The system has an identity now that isn't "agent framework" or "orchestration tool." It's a governance system for shared commons where the governed are ephemeral. That's a genuinely novel problem statement — Ostrom never had community members who forget between meetings. The coordination layer (exploration mode, cross-agent synthesis, context routing) is where investment should go. The compliance layer works and is measurable but asymptotes. External literature confirms this from multiple angles (Google scaling paper, MAST taxonomy, Cursor architecture, Team of Rivals), but nobody is addressing cross-session coordination — our differentiator.
