## Session Synthesis

**Thread:** This session was about discovering what the product surface actually needs to be. We started with naming, but the real work was two threads that formed and converged.

**Insight:** The product isn't just about what you know (threads, briefs, tensions) — it's also about what you should do. Two new comprehension surfaces emerged: shape classification ("what kind of move is this?") and resistance signal ("is this working or fighting you?"). These answer different questions than the first three but are equally product-defining. The key connection was to behavioral grammars — the routing grammar that helps you find the right first move should be legible, not hidden. And the resistance signal (fix count going up vs down) is already in the data but invisible to the human.

**Position:** The product surface is five elements. The first three existed. The last two were discovered this session through conversation, not investigation. The next question is experiential: "what does it feel like when all five are working?" — that bridges architecture to naming.
