# Publication sequencing — each piece creates demand for the next

## 2026-03-10

The trap with 'do it all' is shipping everything at 70% where nothing becomes the thing people reach for. The version that works: each piece creates demand for the next. (1) Publication first — names the problem, people recognize it. (2) Tooling second — 'try it' button in the same news cycle as the essay. The Rails play: essay + scaffold together. (3) kb-cli third — solution to a problem most people haven't had yet, but the first two pieces create that audience. The difference between 'do it all' that works and 'do it all' that dissipates: sequential demand creation vs three independent launches competing for attention.
