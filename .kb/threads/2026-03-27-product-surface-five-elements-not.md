# The product surface is five elements, not three — shape and resistance join threads, briefs, and tensions

## 2026-03-27

Two threads converge. The shape-routing thread ('intent arrives without shape') discovered that the system should help the human see what kind of move they're making — search, convergence, forming, execution. The resistance thread ('resistance is the system's opinion') discovered that the system should help the human distinguish bolted-on work (fix count goes up) from just-hard work (fix count goes down). Both are comprehension surfaces that don't exist yet, and both are computable from data the system already has. Combined with the three elements from the minimum comprehension surface investigation (threads, briefs, tensions), the product surface is five elements: (1) What you're thinking about — threads, (2) What was learned — briefs, (3) What's still open — tensions, (4) What kind of move is this? — shape classification, (5) Is this working or fighting you? — resistance signal. The first three answer 'what do I know?' The last two answer 'what should I do?' — which is the pre-cycle moment where cognitive load actually lives.

Next session opener: 'What does this product feel like to use when it's working?' The five elements are architecture — internal language. The naming question and method guide both need the experience described from the outside. What's the sentence you'd say in a room? This bridges product surface work (what to build) with positioning (what to call it). The writing instinct — story first, earn the abstraction — is the right tool for this question.
