# Epistemic status of AI behavioral experiments — discovering emergent properties, training echoes, or substrate-general coordination laws?

## 2026-03-26

Three competing explanations for AI agent attractor/gate dynamics: (1) Training echo — agents reproduce human constraint responses, AI is just the measurement instrument. (2) Architectural — transformer attention mechanics produce the effects. (3) Substrate-general — coordination law for any bounded-rationality agent. These predict different boundaries and different engineering implications.

First experimental result: null finding. Haiku follows 20 simultaneous orthogonal constraints at ~97%. Prior 'dilution at 10+' claim was observational from production where constraints genuinely interfere. The agent isn't failing from architectural limits — it fails when facing genuine tension between competing instructions. Looks more like bounded-rationality response to impossible demands than training echo or architectural limit. Next: tension-based constraints to test if degradation returns when constraints actually compete.

## 2026-03-27

Training confound analysis: the question "is the LLM discovering or remembering?" may be structurally unresolvable. Three partial strategies exist (non-LLM agents, novel domains, predictive power), but only predictive power is tractable. Full resolution blocked by observational equivalence — correct retrieval and independent discovery produce identical outputs. The bounded-rationality framing dissolves the confound: problems with few viable solutions produce convergence regardless of how agents arrive at solutions. The distinction between "law" and "small solution space" is the real question, and it doesn't require resolving the training confound. See: `2026-03-27-training-confound-resolvability.md`.
