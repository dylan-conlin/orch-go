# Distribution as a substrate — does the theory apply to its own adoption?

## 2026-03-10

Good posts with no audience infrastructure = orphaned investigations with no model connection. The five conditions: (1) multiple writers publishing to the internet, (2) no cross-session memory — audiences don't remember you between posts, (3) each post is locally correct, (4) posts must compose into a recognizable body of work, (5) no structural coordination — no audience, no presence, no distribution infrastructure. All five hold. Prediction: without attractors (consistent presence in specific communities) and structural coupling (repeated engagement), posts scatter. Open question: is this a genuine substrate instance or a cute analogy? The test is whether the prescribed interventions (attractors, gates, entropy measurement) actually work for distribution the way they work for code and knowledge.

CLOSED: built on unvalidated knowledge-accretion premise. 'Does the theory apply to its own adoption?' presupposes the theory is valid.
