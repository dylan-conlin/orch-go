# Models as proving ground — principles are what survives when models become obsolete

## 2026-03-27

Models are working hypotheses about specific domains — they describe how something works, grounded in evidence from this system. Principles are decision guides that transfer across domains. The relationship: models incubate candidate principles as invariants or claims. When an invariant shows up across domains the original model didn't anticipate, it graduates. Absent Signal Trap lived as measurement-honesty invariant #2 for two weeks before appearing independently in knowledge queries and agent self-reports — that cross-domain recurrence was the promotion signal. The other direction matters too: not everything in a model becomes a principle. 'Enforcement and measurement must be paired' and 'clustering vs synthesis is a boundary choice' are still model-level. They might graduate or might stay as domain-specific knowledge. The test is whether they keep showing up where you don't expect them.
