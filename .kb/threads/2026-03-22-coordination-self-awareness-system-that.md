# Coordination self-awareness — the system that knows when coordination is making things worse

## 2026-03-22

Convergence of 5 open threads (narrative-packaging, constraint-first-design, silent-cost-accumulation, open-loop-systems, comprehension-gated-throughput, coordination-protocol-primitives) into one claim: Coordination systems fail when they cannot observe their own consequences. The four primitives (Route/Sequence/Throttle/Align) describe WHAT needs coordinating. Open-loop describes WHY it fails. Silent cost/behavioral accretion describes HOW the failure manifests. The design response: less machinery, not more. Constraint surfaces beat orchestration when you can shrink the action space. Comprehension-gating is the human equivalent -- slow down to understanding speed because throughput without comprehension is waste (777 orphans prove this). orch-go evidence: 38 stale decisions, 42 hotspots, 776 orphan investigations are the system own coordination accreting exactly like code does. Candidate narrative hook: I built a system to orchestrate AI agents, and it taught me that most orchestration should not exist. Adjacent: coordination-protocol-primitives (the primitives are real and general, this thread is about what happens when they accrete).

## 2026-03-23

Experiments (269 trials) surfaced three layers of coordination failure, each with a different solution: (1) ignorance — agents have false merge models, fixable by education (~30% recovery), (2) gravitational bias — agents prefer correct over safe, fixable by structural placement, (3) shared infrastructure — imports/config are irreducibly shared, only solvable by N=1 or post-hoc reconciliation. The sharper question: not 'how do we coordinate better' but 'how do we decompose work so coordination becomes trivial or unnecessary.' This reframes the model from coordination theory to decomposition theory.
