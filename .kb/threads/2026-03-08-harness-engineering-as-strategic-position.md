# Harness engineering as strategic position — build → publish → tooling

## 2026-03-08

Strategic position crystallized: we have the pain (3 spirals, 1,625 lost commits), the evidence (265 contrastive trials, daemon.go +892 / spawn_cmd.go -840), the working system (5 layers, 46 completions/day), and the vocabulary (hard/soft harness, attractors + gates, routing table, accretion as thermodynamics). Nobody else has all four. Sequence: keep building (answer open questions, get Layer 4, cross-language evidence) → publish with unassailable evidence → ship portable tooling. Consulting slots in anywhere as funding + research source. Key risk: someone publishes a shallower version first. Mitigant: depth survives shallow competition — shallow frameworks get adopted then abandoned. The open questions (governance health metric, soft harness budget curve, self-extending gates, cross-language portability) are the moat-deepening work.

Key reframe: two distinct failure modes — compliance failure (agents don't follow instructions, solved by stronger models) vs coordination failure (agents each follow instructions correctly but collectively produce entropy, made WORSE by stronger models). daemon.go +892 lines was coordination failure, not compliance failure. Implication: compliance gates (Layer 0-1) simplify with model improvement, but coordination gates (Layer 2-4) become more important. Harness engineering is permanent discipline, not transitional. Publication claim shifts from 'preventing agent mistakes' to 'coordination infrastructure that capable agents require' — same reason Google has architecture review despite hiring top engineers.
