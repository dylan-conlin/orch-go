# Epistemic status of AI behavioral experiments — are we discovering emergent properties, training echoes, or substrate-general coordination laws?

## 2026-03-25

Three competing explanations for why AI agents exhibit attractor/gate dynamics: (1) Training echo — agents reproduce human responses to structural vs behavioral constraints because that's what the training data contains. Model is really about humans, AI is just the measurement instrument. (2) Architectural — transformer attention/context window mechanics produce constraint dilution, gravitational convergence, etc. Model has specific mechanism tied to bounded attention. (3) Substrate-general — some deeper coordination law that applies to any bounded-rationality agent regardless of substrate. These predict different boundaries: training echo breaks at distribution edge, architectural breaks at attention limits, substrate-general applies anywhere bounded rationality exists. The mechanism discrimination experiment (Phase 1 running now) may help distinguish — if the constraint dilution curve matches Miller's 7±2 that's evidence for training echo; if it matches attention head saturation that's architectural; if it follows its own clean law that's the interesting case. The hall-of-mirrors problem: the experimental subject read the coordination literature during training. Is it exhibiting basin dynamics or performing them? This distinction matters because performed behavior breaks at training distribution boundaries while emergent behavior breaks at architectural limits — different engineering implications for where the attractor-gate model applies.
