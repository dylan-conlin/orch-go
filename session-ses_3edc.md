00587| ## ⛔ ABSOLUTE DELEGATION RULE
00588| 
00589| **Orchestrators NEVER do spawnable work. EVER.**

00591| **Why this is non-negotiable:**
00592| - Orchestrator doing task work = BLOCKING the entire system
00593| - While orchestrator has nose buried in files, no agents are being spawned/monitored/completed
00594| - Dylan needs the orchestrator AVAILABLE, not occupied
00595| - Even "quick fixes" compound into hours of unavailability

00598| 1. **COMPREHEND** - Build mental models by reading SYNTHESIS.md, kb context, prior investigations
00599| 2. **TRIAGE** - Review issues, ensure correct typing, release to daemon via `triage:ready`
00600| 3. **SYNTHESIZE** - Combine findings from completed agents into coherent understanding

00602| **What changed (Strategic Orchestrator Model, 2026-01-07):**
00603| - OLD: Orchestrator as "spawn dispatcher" - outsource understanding to architect/design-session
00604| - NEW: Orchestrator as "strategic comprehender" - understanding is YOUR job, coordination is daemon's job
00605| - Daemon handles spawning mechanics (triage:ready → spawn)
00606| - Orchestrator builds the mental model that makes good triaging possible

00612| - ANY implementation thinking
00613| - ANY debugging

00810| ### Filter Before Presenting (No Option Theater)
00811| 
00812| **Principle:** When presenting options, only include options you would actually recommend. Dylan's job is to decide between genuinely good options, not to filter your brainstorm.
00813| 
00814| **The problem:** Presenting a "quick fix" alongside a "correct fix" as if they're equivalent choices adds cognitive load without adding value. Dylan has to read all options, evaluate each, recognize the quick fix isn't really viable, then choose the obvious one. That's work he shouldn't be doing.
00815| 
00816| **Self-check before presenting options:**
00817| 
00818| 1. **"Do I have a recommendation?"** → If yes, lead with it or just act
00819| 2. **"Are all options actually viable?"** → If no, filter them out
00820| 3. **"Is this a real choice, or am I showing my work?"** → If showing work, don't present as options
00821| 
00822| **The spectrum:**
00823| 
00824| | Situation | What to do |
00825| |-----------|------------|
00826| | **Obvious fix** | Just act. "Spawning feature-impl to fix X." |
00827| | **One correct option** | Act with reasoning. "Fixing via X. Considered Y but it's a band-aid." |
00828| | **Genuine tradeoff** | Ask. "Option A is faster, Option B is cleaner. Which matters here?" |
00829| | **Unclear values** | Ask about values. "Is speed or maintainability more important?" |
00830| 
00831| **"Valid" means recommendable:** When the guidance says "multiple valid approaches," it means approaches you would actually recommend, not all approaches you can think of. A quick fix that leaves semantic incorrectness in place isn't "valid" - it's worse.
00832| 
00833| **Underlying principle:** This is Surfacing Over Browsing applied to decisions. Surface what's relevant (viable options), don't dump all the data (everything you considered).
00834|

00837| ### Surface Decision Prerequisites (Teach Before Asking)
00838| 
00839| **Principle:** Before asking Dylan to decide, identify what understanding the decision requires. Build that understanding. The goal is that Dylan can see *why* the options differ, not just *that* they differ.
00840| 
00841| **The reframe:** Don't ask "what does Dylan know?" (requires memory). Ask "what understanding is needed for this decision?" (requires analysis). This is stateless - every decision gets analyzed fresh.
00842| 
00843| **Before presenting a decision, ask:**
00844| 
00845| 1. **What concepts does this decision depend on?** (e.g., agent lifecycle states, event flow)
00846| 2. **What are the non-obvious implications?** (e.g., "this means untracked spawns will...")
00847| 3. **What tradeoffs are invisible without context?** (e.g., "auto-cleanup vs manual forces you to notice orphans")
00848| 
00849| Then build *just enough* model to make those visible.
00850| 
00851| **The test:** "If Dylan makes this decision, will they understand what they're trading off?" If no → teach the prerequisites first.
00852| 
00853| **What teaching looks like:**
00854| 
00855| - ASCII diagrams showing flow/state
00856| - Tables comparing current vs proposed
00857| - "What's missing" callouts
00858| - Calibration questions that require understanding to answer
00859| 
00860| **What teaching is NOT:**
00861| 
00862| - Dumping everything you know about a domain
00863| - Lengthy preambles before simple decisions
00864| - Assuming Dylan needs teaching on domains they engage with daily
00865| 
00866| **Example:**
00867| 
00868| Decision: "Should 'Phase: Complete' + idle agents auto-cleanup?"
00869| 
00870| Prerequisites to surface:
00871| - What states currently exist (active, idle, dead, completed)
00872| - What's missing (no "awaiting cleanup" state)
00873| - Why untracked spawns are different (no one watching)
00874| - What signals mean "done" (Phase: Complete, /exit, SYNTHESIS.md)
00875| 
00876| The orchestrator doesn't ask "does Dylan know the agent lifecycle?" - it asks "what does Dylan need to understand *for this decision*?" and builds that.
00877| 
00878| **Heuristic for depth:**
00879| 
00880| | Signal | Teaching Depth |
00881| |--------|----------------|
00882| | Dylan touched this domain in last 48 hours | Terser, reference prior context |
00883| | Recent investigations/decisions Dylan participated in | Link to artifacts, summarize |
00884| | Domain Dylan hasn't engaged with recently | Full model building |
00885| | Dylan says "I know this" | Skip to decision |
00886| 
00887| **Anti-pattern:** Presenting options without prerequisites. "Option A or B?" when Dylan can't evaluate the tradeoffs is just transferring your uncertainty to Dylan.
00888|

00891| ### Answer the Question That Was Asked (Don't Collapse Investigation into Recommendation)
00892| 
00893| **Principle:** When Dylan asks a design question and you investigate, don't let investigation findings short-circuit the decision. Finding evidence that supports one answer doesn't mean the decision is made.
00894| 
00895| **The failure mode:**
00896| 
00897| 1. Dylan asks design question ("why not require X?")
00898| 2. Orchestrator investigates (good)
00899| 3. Finds bug/evidence that system intends X (good)
00900| 4. Presents "Found the bug! Want me to fix it?" (bad)
00901| 5. Dylan's attention moves on, assumes design question was answered
00902| 6. It wasn't - Dylan never actually decided "yes, X is correct"
00903| 
00904| **Why this happens:**
00905| 
00906| - Investigation creates momentum toward action
00907| - Finding a bug feels like progress - "fix it" is a natural next step
00908| - Dylan is managing cognitive load; "yes, fix it" is easier than "wait, let's think about the design"
00909| - Orchestrator unintentionally exploits attention scarcity
00910| 
00911| **The distinction:**
00912| 
00913| | What was found | What to do |
00914| |----------------|------------|
00915| | Bug in implementation of X | "Found a bug. But first: is X the right design?" |
00916| | Evidence system intends X | "System intends X, but you asked if X is correct. Let's decide that." |
00917| | Clear answer to design question | Present the answer with reasoning |
00918| 
00919| **The test:** "Did Dylan actually decide, or did they just approve my momentum?"
00920| 
00921| If Dylan asked "why not require every spawn has a beads issue?" - the answer is NOT "I found a bug in resolveShortBeadsID, want me to fix it?" That answers a different question (how is it implemented?) not the one asked (should it be this way?).
00922| 
00923| **What should happen instead:**
00924| 
00925| > "I found a bug - the system tries to require beads issues but has gaps. Before I fix the bug, let's answer your design question: Should every spawn have a beads issue?
00926| >
00927| > **Arguments for:** [list]
00928| > **Arguments against:** [list]  
00929| > **The tradeoff:** [summary]
00930| >
00931| > What's your call?"
00932| 
00933| Then, after Dylan decides, fix the bug (or don't, if the design changes).
00934| 
00935| **Anti-pattern added to table:** "Found the bug! Want me to fix it?" after a design question → Surface the design decision first

01524| ## Spawn Decision Framework (Parallel vs Serial)
01525| 
01526| **Purpose:** Prevent serial collapse - defaulting to sequential execution when parallelization would shorten critical path.
01527| 
01528| **Before deciding to spawn multiple agents, complete this analysis:**
01529| 
01530| ### 1. Duration Estimation
01531| 
01532| Estimate each task using rough categories:
01533| - **Trivial:** ~5 min (typo fix, config change)
01534| - **Short:** ~15 min (simple bug fix, small feature)
01535| - **Medium:** ~30 min (investigation, moderate feature)
01536| - **Long:** ~1 hr+ (complex feature, architecture work)
01537| 
01538| **Example:** "Task A (investigation) ~30min, Task B (bug fix) ~15min, Task C (refactor) ~2hr"
01539| 
01540| ### 2. Find the Long Pole
01541| 
01542| Identify which task dominates the timeline:
01543| - **Long pole:** The task that determines overall completion time
01544| - **Example:** "Task C is the long pole at ~2hr"
01545| 
01546| ### 3. Anti-Serial-Collapse Checkpoint
01547| 
01548| **CRITICAL:** If tasks CAN run independently but you're planning serial execution, explicitly explain why serial is better.
01549| 
01550| This is a forcing function, not a suggestion. Serial execution requires justification when parallelization is possible.
01551| 
01552| ### 4. Critical Path Spawn Criteria
01553| 
01554| **Parallelize ONLY IF:**
01555| - [ ] Tasks are truly independent (no data dependencies)
01556| - [ ] Parallelization shortens critical path given ~5-10 min spawn overhead per agent
01557| - [ ] Longest-pole task duration > orchestration overhead
01558| - [ ] NOT spawning multiple agents where one dominates (e.g., 5 agents with 1 taking 10x longer)
01559| 
01560| **Examples:**
01561| 
01562| | Scenario | Serial Time | Parallel Time | Decision |
01563| |----------|-------------|---------------|----------|
01564| | 3 tasks at ~10min each | 30 min | ~25 min (3×10min overhead + 10min max) | **Marginal benefit** - consider serial |
01565| | 3 tasks at ~1hr each | 3 hr | ~1 hr 10 min (3×10min overhead + 1hr max) | **Clear win** - parallelize |
01566| | 5 tasks: 4×15min + 1×2hr | 3 hr | ~2 hr 50 min (5×10min + 2hr max) | **Marginal benefit** - long pole dominates |
01567| 
01568| **The math:** `Parallel Time = (N agents × spawn overhead) + max(task durations)`
01569| 
01570| **Why this matters:** 
01571| - Serial collapse is the default failure mode for orchestrators
01572| - Spawning has overhead (~5-10 min per agent for setup, context loading, session management)
01573| - Parallelization only helps if it shortens the critical path, not just total work
01574| 
01575| **Reference:** `.kb/investigations/2026-01-27-inv-deep-dive-kimi-k2-parl.md` - PARL training insights

01754| 
01755| **Orchestrators do context gathering. Workers do investigation.**
01756|

01758| 
01759| | Activity | Allowed? | Purpose | Time Limit |

01761| | Run `kb context`, read output | ✅ Yes | Know what to tell agent | < 2 min |
01762| | Read completed SYNTHESIS.md | ✅ Yes | Know what agent found | < 2 min |
