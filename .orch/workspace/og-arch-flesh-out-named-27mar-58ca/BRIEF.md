# Brief: orch-go-w9dgz

## Frame

The named-incompleteness model was the first thread-to-model promotion in the system. Three threads had converged on a single idea — incompleteness is generative, named incompleteness is the engine — but the model file was an empty scaffold with just the thread lineage pasted in. The question wasn't whether the idea was right (the external validation probe had already confirmed it across Luhmann, Popper, Kuhn). The question was whether "subsumes three child models" was overclaiming, and if not, what precisely the subsumption means.

## Resolution

The core mechanism turned out to be cleaner than I expected: gaps are specific coordinates in possibility space, conclusions are generic. Two questions about the same gap converge automatically (they share a coordinate). Two conclusions about different findings sit next to each other inert. This is the same reason across every substrate — agent briefs, scientific papers, Zettelkasten notes, open source issues, code packages. It's information-theoretic, not analogical.

The subsumption claim needed careful work. I read all three child models in full and mapped each one against NI's core prediction. The result: NI subsumes at the "why" level — it makes the same core prediction in each domain, for the same reason. But the child models have 15+ independent predictions that NI doesn't touch. Compositional accretion adds the design criteria and adoption mechanics. Attractor-gate adds the timing dimension (design-time vs runtime — a genuinely independent insight). Knowledge accretion adds the measurement frameworks and second-order pathologies. "Subsumes" means "provides the unifying explanation" — not "replaces."

The part I found most interesting: attractor-gate is the weakest subsumption. NI explains why attractors work (they're named gaps), but WHY design-time embedding beats runtime enforcement isn't fully captured by "gaps are coordinates." The AG model has its own independent insight about when coordination must happen, not just what it's made of.

## Tension

The model is entirely retrodictive. Every piece of evidence comes from explaining observations already made. NI-03 predicts bibliometric gap-based clustering should outperform conclusion-based clustering — that's the most concrete novel prediction and nobody has tested it. Until a prediction is generated THEN confirmed, this is pattern recognition, not science. The question for Dylan: is a retrodictive meta-model that unifies three domain models useful as a design principle even before it's predictively validated?
